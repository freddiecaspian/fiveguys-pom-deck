Every morning, each store is handed one forecast number. Ours was €28,000. Everything downstream — patties, potatoes, labour — is a deterministic conversion from it.

NEWSVENDOR WORKING
Cu / (Cu + Co)  ≈  0.99    ←  critical ratio
μ   =  €28,000             ←  mean daily demand
σ   =  ±€7,000             ←  current forecast sigma
Q*  ≈  €50,000 prep        ←  rational response at 99% service
STRUCTURAL 80% OVERSHOOT. EVERY DAY. BY DESIGN.
Five Guys can't change Cu. Can't change Co. The only lever is σ.

03
HOPE  ×  SOLUTION

You don't need a better model.
You need a tournament.

Generative AI turns Five Guys' 1m+ labelled store-days into a permanent forecasting capability.

10 YEARS × 294 STORES = ~1,000,000 LABELLED STORE-DAYS
forecast vs. actual, every day, every store.
STAGE 01
HUNDREDS OF MODELS

Disney park attendance · weather · school holidays · hotel occupancy · local events · lagged POS · football fixtures · public holidays · day-of-week · weather forecasts · concert schedules · competitor proximity · …

STAGE 02
SCORED DAILY

Each model makes a prediction. Each one scored against actual revenue at close of business. Every single day.

STAGE 03
WINNERS DEPLOYED

Promote the winners. Retire the losers. Per store, per cluster — different stores learn different winning models. Disney Village weights park attendance. London City weights office workers.

POM MECHANISM

This is a sigma-reduction strategy analogous to pre-sales information (Session 07) — a demand-side real option. Same Cu, same Co, same critical ratio. Tighter Q*. Lower expected cost on every single day, not just the tails.
